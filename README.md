## **Interpreting User Needs**

I interpret user needs by breaking them down into clear, actionable requirements that describe what the user wants to accomplish and why it matters. Creating [user stories](copilot-action://composer-send?text=Explain%20how%20user%20stories%20help%20interpret%20user%20needs) helps with this because each story captures the user’s goal, the value behind it, and the acceptance criteria that define success. This structure ensures that development stays focused on delivering meaningful functionality rather than just completing tasks.

## **My Approach to Program Development**

When developing programs, I follow an iterative mindset that emphasizes small, incremental improvements. I plan to incorporate Agile practices such as [Sprint Planning](copilot-action://composer-send?text=What%20happens%20in%20Sprint%20Planning), [Daily Scrums](copilot-action://composer-send?text=Describe%20the%20Daily%20Scrum), and continuous testing into my future work. These processes help maintain clarity, adaptability, and steady progress. Agile allows me to respond to change quickly and ensures that feedback is integrated throughout the development cycle rather than at the end.

## **Being a Good Team Member**

Being a good team member in software development means communicating clearly, collaborating respectfully, and supporting the team’s shared goals. It involves writing maintainable code, offering help when teammates are blocked, and being open to feedback. A strong team member also participates actively in Scrum events like [Sprint Reviews](copilot-action://composer-send?text=What%20is%20a%20Sprint%20Review) and [Retrospectives](copilot-action://composer-send?text=Explain%20the%20purpose%20of%20a%20Sprint%20Retrospective), contributing to continuous improvement and a positive team culture.
